IT for local farms.

Coverage that reaches the barn and the back field. Mobile workflows that hold up at the farmers market. Email that won't get you spoofed when a wholesale buyer is paying on terms.

What farms actually need

Farms are small businesses with infrastructure problems most SMBs don't have. Rural bandwidth, outbuildings, freezer alerts, weather-station data, and a tablet that has to work in the rain. We do practical setups that survive the conditions and don't lock you into a SaaS the moment you turn the truck off.

Connectivity

  • Site surveys and Wi-Fi remediation for barns, packing sheds, and outbuildings
  • Cellular failover for the office when the wireline goes out (which it will)
  • Remote sensors for cold storage, irrigation, or anything with a reading you'd rather know about before it fails

Market day & mobile

  • Point-of-sale setups that work on flaky cell coverage
  • Sync between markets, farm stand, and the back office without manual reconciliation
  • Mobile device management so a dropped phone doesn't compromise the books

Wholesale & B2B

  • Email authentication so chefs, distributors, and grocers can trust your invoices
  • Secure document sharing for orders and product sheets
  • Simple automations for order intake, packing lists, and delivery notes

Self-hosted where it fits

Some farm tools (irrigation, sensors, cameras) work better when they don't depend on a third-party SaaS that may not exist in three years. When self-hosted is the right call, we run it on small, reliable hardware with backups handled properly.
