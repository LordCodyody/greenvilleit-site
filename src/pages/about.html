I run this practice as a single operator on purpose. SMBs deserve senior-level security thinking — the kind that lives at the CISO desk of a larger company — applied to their actual problems instead of being filed under a junior tech's ticket queue.

That means you talk to the person doing the work. There's no account executive layer between you and a fix, no SDR cold-calling to upsell you, no quarterly stack review where a vendor tries to talk you into a product you don't need.

What we believe

Security comes first, but only the parts that matter.

Most SMBs are not losing sleep over nation-state APTs. They are losing sleep over business email compromise, spoofed invoices, a former employee who still has access to a shared mailbox, and the laptop that grew legs at a trade show. We work on the things that actually happen.

No vendor lock-in pitches.

We're platform-agnostic. We've shipped real work on both Microsoft 365 and Google Workspace. When we recommend a tool, it's because it fits, not because we're a reseller. Our incentive is to make your stack work — not to keep you on a renewal cycle.

Small is a feature.

A solo practice can move on a single conversation. You don't file a ticket and wait for it to be routed. You don't get rotated through three techs who each re-ask the same five questions. Engagement scope is what we can deliver well, deliberately limited so quality doesn't slip.

Practical over impressive.

We'd rather ship a boring DMARC remediation that stops your domain from being spoofed than deploy a flashy AI dashboard that nobody opens twice. We say no to projects where AI is the wrong answer.
